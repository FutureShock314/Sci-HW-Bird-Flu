Bird Flu

(Well, infection anyway.) Identifying bird flu is obviously the first step in fighting it, except not getting it in the first place, so it's important to know the symptoms. Symtoms of bird flue include: swollen heads, a blue colouration of the comb and wattles, dullness, lack of appetite, respiratory distress, diarrhoea and significant drop in egg production. Aside from noticing these, the best defense against flu is to not get it. This can be done by following some simple steps: keep birds in safe/fenced areas, keep your birds from contact with wild birds, and keep food in secure areas. However, bird flu isn't exclusive to birds (I know, right?). It can also be transmitted to cats, dogs, and other pets. It can be gained through eating of a bird with the disease, or coming into contact with said bird with said disease. It can also infect humans, but only very occasionally. Symptoms range from a mild flu like illness, sometimes with conjunctivitis (red, sore, discharging eyes), diarrhoea and abdominal pain, to a severe respiratory illness with breathing difficulties and pneumonia.
